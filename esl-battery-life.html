ESL Battery Life Explained: Why 7-Year Labels Change Everything

Mar 12, 2026·6 min read·AiESL Research

Electronic shelf labels last 5 to 10 years on a single battery because e-paper draws power only when the image changes. Between updates, the label holds its content with effectively no energy — the physics is what makes long life possible.

Why e-paper sips power

Unlike an LCD or LED screen that must be lit continuously, e-paper is bistable: once pixels flip to show an image, they stay there with no power. A label only consumes energy during the brief moment it updates, and during short radio wake-ups to listen for changes. The rest of the time it draws almost nothing.

The key number
A typical AiESL label runs 5–10 years on a single battery at 2–4 updates per day — verified at a stated update frequency, not a best-case lab figure.

What actually determines lifespan

  • Update frequency — the single biggest factor. Three changes a day uses far less energy than dozens.
  • Radio activity — how often the label wakes to check for updates and the efficiency of the protocol.
  • Display size & color — larger and multi-color refreshes move more pixels and cost more energy per update.
  • Battery capacity — bigger labels carry more or larger cells to match their higher per-update draw.
  • Temperature — extreme cold reduces battery performance, which is why cold-chain labels use sealed designs.

Why battery life is a cost issue

Battery replacement is field labor — staff time multiplied across every label in every store. A label that lasts 7 years instead of 3 more than halves that recurring cost over a deployment’s life. When comparing vendors, always ask for the verified figure at a stated update rate, because a best-case number with no frequency attached is meaningless.

Reading a spec sheet honestly

Look for two things: the update frequency the life figure assumes, and whether the battery is replaceable. AiESL publishes life at 2–4 updates/day and offers replaceable battery packs on most series; see the products page for per-model figures.
