How Much Do Electronic Shelf Labels Cost? Pricing Breakdown

Apr 22, 2026·6 min read·AiESL Research

There is no single ESL price because a deployment is a system, not a product. The per-label hardware cost is only one line; gateways, software, integration and install round out the budget. Here is how each piece scales.

The four cost components

ComponentWhat it coversHow it scales
LabelsThe e-paper tags themselvesBy size & color & quantity
Base stationsGateways that drive the labelsBy floor area & ceiling height
Software / platformManagement console & AI enginePer store / subscription
Integration & installERP/POS connectors, mountingOne-time per rollout

What drives label price

Three factors dominate: size (a 1.54″ hook tag costs far less than a 10.2″ display), color (black/white/red/yellow is standard; full 7-color costs more), and quantity (price per label falls sharply at volume; watch minimum order quantities).

How many base stations you need

Gateways are sized by coverage, not SKU count. One base station covers a radius that shrinks with mounting height — a unit on a high ceiling reaches a smaller floor footprint than its rated range. A large warehouse needs more gateways than a compact store of the same SKU count. Estimate yours with the base station coverage planner.

Budget tip
Reusing existing labels through open middleware, and deploying premium color or large-format tags only where they earn their keep, can cut a rollout budget by more than half.

Build a realistic per-store budget

Start from SKU count and store size: labels scale with SKUs, gateways with floor area, software with store count, install is one-time. Then offset against the labor and paper you stop spending. The ROI calculator turns those inputs into a payback period and three-year net savings.
